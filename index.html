Purpose

Through AcademiaEdge we hope to provide an alternative to expensive programming classes by offering completely free, accessible, and high-quality coding courses.

Created By

High Schoolers and University Students who strive to make education more equitable. We have advanced expertise in web development and programming, as well as a passion for education and learning.

Values

We promise to be responsible for the students we teach, giving them the highest quality education possible. Our course instructors are selected through a vigorous interview process. We value integrity, privacy, and honesty in our classes and workplace.

Structure

In order to give an individualized experience, each class has two teachers and is limited to 10-15 students. Each class will be held weekly over Zoom with course materials posted in Google Classroom. Once a course is full a waitlist will be created.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What's the price for a class?

    Free! We are a non-profit organization, which means that our program is 100% free of charge.

  • Who can sign-up?

    Anyone! Our classes are free making it accessible for anyone and everyone to get involved or learn a new programming language.

  • What platform do you use for classes?

    Our classes are over Zoom and we use Google Classroom to post class materials, assign homework, and make important announcements.

  • Are you looking for new teachers?

    Yes, we are always looking to find new teachers and organizers for AcademiaEdge. If you are interested with partnering with us please visit the Join Us page!

  • What do you teach?

    Our courses are focused on programming and coding. We offer a variety of computer science related courses.

  • How many classes do you have?

    Currently we offer 15 unique and well-crafted courses and are always looking towards expanding our selection.

  • How long are your classes?

    Our classes range from 45 minutes to an hour long.

  • Is there homework for students?

    Yes, every week teachers will post homework which students are expected to complete and submit. In the following week, we will grade and go over the homework in class.
